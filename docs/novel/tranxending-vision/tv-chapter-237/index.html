The dinner was plentiful but not exactly delicious; Xia Lei ate happily. Zhou Xiao-Hong’s parents were simple farmers and Xia Lei felt like he had returned to the times at the construction site with their talk about rice, oil and salt; it was a warm dinner table. 

Cherishing the past enabled one to cherish the present. He was not the sort who would think highly of himself now that he had gained some power. 

After dinner, Xia Lei and Zhou Xiao-Hong discussed what they would do tomorrow when going to the government office to talk about investing in the factory. He felt it rather odd but he could not figure out why after pondering the question for quite a while. 

Rain fell outside the window and raindrops rolled along the lines of the eaves, dropping to the ground with a pitter-patter. The climate in the basin was strange - the rain did not wet clothing but when it started it seemed it would go on for the whole day without end. The sound of rain was hypnotic and slowly, gradually, Xia Lei closed his eyes and went to sleep.

Zhou Xiao-Hong’s face was red. “A burglar did come in but nothing was stolen. I think that burglar’s very kind and couldn’t bear to steal.”

Xia Lei couldn’t help laughing. Zhou Xiao-Hong cracked jokes too but it was fortunate that she could joke about something like this. Last night was in the past and everyone was going to pretend nothing happened - this was good.

After breakfast, Xia Lei went with Zhou Xiao-Hong to the local government office. The economy was in recession and few businesses were moving to expand. It took considerable effort on the government’s part to get investors interested but Xia Lei had come knocking all on his own so it was a happy event for the local government. 

The official in charge of taking care of Xia Lei and Zhou Xiao-Hong was a man called Shang Hai, the director of the local development office. After gaining an understanding of their situation, he said, “If you’re opening a branch company, you just have to do some simple procedures. I can help you finish it all today if you have all the materials ready. It will take longer if you’re starting a brand new company, but it won’t take more than three days.” 

Xia Lei had also been wavering between a new company or a branch company. He thought for a bit and asked probingly, “I’ll be honest, Director Shang. I want to move my company somewhere else because someone is throttling my business back home. Will people from the Trade and Commerce Bureau come seal my factory off if I build one here?” 

Shang Hai looked blankly at him, then laughed. “Are you pulling my leg, Mr Xia? Our industrial development zone is a project which was re-established after a catastrophe. These benefits are also from the government - who dares mess with it unless he wants to have his meals in a prison cell? You can rest assured, Mr Xia. As long as your company is running a legal business I can guarantee that no one would dare make trouble for you here.” 

This was just a verbal promise but Xia Lei relaxed quite a bit when he heard it. “Good then, it’s decided. I’m going to open a branch company. The procedures are simpler too if I do it this way. I actually already have my eye on a piece of land in your industrial development zone. I prefer things to be straightforward too so let’s get started on those procedures.” 

Shang Hai got up and shook hands with Xia Lei, then said, “Please hold on, Mr Xia. I’ll give our Mayor a call.” 

“Please, go ahead,” said Xia Lei politely. 

After Shang Hai left the room, Zhou Xiao-Hong said, “Aren’t you going to think about it more, Big Brother Lei? It’s a big decision.” 

“There’s nothing to consider. There are excellent benefits here and it saves money and trouble. I was going to open a branch company anyway so why not here? It’s close to your home too so you can take care of your family when you work here,” said Xia Lei. 

“Will you still come here?” asked Zhou Xiao-Hong in a small voice. 

Xia Lei smiled. “This is my company so how can I not come?” 

Zhou Xiao-Hong smiled then. “I’ll be here watching over your company then.” 

“I’ll get Yin Hao to come here to help you. You’re the head and he shall be your deputy. He is capable and you can just manage him. You understand what I mean?”

Zhou Xiao-Hong nodded, afraid to miss any word of his. 

Just then, Shang Hai walked in with a smile on his face. “Mr Xia, let’s go view the piece of land you have your eye on. Our Major is already on his way to the industrial development zone.” 

Xia Lei stood. “All right. After you.”
